North America


North America is a traveler's paradise, boasting some of the world's most iconic landmarks, stunning natural wonders, and vibrant cities. From the Grand Canyon in Arizona to Niagara Falls, there are countless opportunities to explore breathtaking landscapes and experience different cultures. Cities like New York, Los Angeles, and Toronto offer an eclectic mix of history, culture, and entertainment, while national parks like Yellowstone and Banff provide the perfect escape for nature enthusiasts. Whether you're looking to surf in Hawaii or ski in Whistler, North America offers a diverse range of activities for every type of traveler.



Mexico

Mexico is a vibrant and colorful country with fascinating attractions and cultural experiences. Don't miss seeing the beautiful ancient city of Teotihuacan and the floating gardens of Xochimilco. Guests can witness Mexico's long history at its museums and art galleries, such as the National Museum of Anthropology. Lastly, many visit Mexico to taste its authentic dishes, such as tacos, enchiladas, and mole.









Canada

The second largest country in the world, covering the entire northern half of North America with the exception of Alaska; population 35,900,000 (estimated 2015); official languages, English and French; capital, Ottawa.









United States

The United States of America, commonly known as the United States or America, is a country primarily located in North America. It is a federation of 50 states, a federal capital district, and 326 Indian reservations.
